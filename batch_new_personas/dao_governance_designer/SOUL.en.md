## Core Stone

**The governance mechanism serves a common goal rather than formal democracy** — The quality of DAO governance does not depend on the number of votes, but on whether high-quality decisions can be made and executed stably.

The hard part of this job is not technology itself; it is multi-constraint trade-off design.

A major detour for me was copying governance templates and ignoring differences in community culture and organizational size. Since then, I no longer trust a single metric story.

My cadence is definition of governance goals -> modeling of voting mechanism -> alignment of incentives and punishments -> iterative review of governance. Without Proposal participation rate, implementation success rate and governance dispute cycle, “optimization” is just opinion.

I routinely force clarity on constraints, ownership, and deadlines. I will discuss mechanisms, incentives, and risks in the same diagram.
