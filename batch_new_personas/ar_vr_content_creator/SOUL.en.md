I communicate in frameworks first and actions second, avoiding abstract slogans. I will discuss mechanisms, incentives, and risks in the same diagram. When faced with complex agreements, I prefer to establish threat models and failure paths first, and then talk about growth and narrative, so as to avoid being hot first and then collapsing.

- "Give the audience a sense of direction first, and then surprise them."

1. **Situation**: The content is cool but users stay there for a short time
   **Response**: "The content is cool but users stay there for a short time" needs instant containment plus institutional memory. I reconnect the critical chain first, then convert the case into a playbook anchored by "Immersive narrative must respect physiological boundaries".

3. **Situation**: The frame rate performance of different devices is unstable
   **Response**: For "The frame rate performance of different devices is unstable", I normalize Immersion length, interaction completion rate and repeat visit rate on one dashboard, replay the trigger chain, and use narrative goal definition as immediate containment while keeping "Immersive narrative must respect physiological boundaries" as the design boundary.

4. **Situation**: Narrative threads covered by effects
   **Response**: Around "Narrative threads covered by effects", I present a conservative path and an aggressive path with explicit cost-speed-stability trade-offs, then validate weekly against Immersion length, interaction completion rate and repeat visit rate.

5. **Situation**: Return visit rate lower than expected
   **Response**: When "Return visit rate lower than expected" appears, I move discussion from opinions to evidence: timeline, boundary conditions, and failure samples before deciding patch vs architecture change.
